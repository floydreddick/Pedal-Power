Pedal Power

What could be more fun than taking to the open road by bike? Riding on two wheels gives you the freedom to see the world from a new perspective. But if you're looking for an extra adrenaline rush, you need to check out the range of extreme biking activities.

Mountain Biking

Mountain biking is the most popular type of extreme biking activity. It includes riding on tracks or trails through off-road terrain such as mountains, forests or snow. Riders typically have to maneuver over roots, rocks, and other obstacles.

Downhill Racing

Downhill racing is the ultimate test of nerve and skill. It’s all about descending a course as quickly as possible. Most downhill courses are made up of jumps, drops, and other natural features, and some also include man made obstacles.

Bike Park Riding

Bike parks are like miniature theme parks designed for mountain bikers. They feature a range of dirt jumps, half pipes, and other stunts. They’re usually located in ski resorts during the summer months, and they’re one of the best places to learn stunts and hone your skills.

BMX

BMX stands for ‘Bicycle Moto-Cross’ and it’s all about performing tricks on a BMX bike. It’s a popular form of extreme biking and it usually involves riding on dirt tracks, skate parks, and other man-made structures.

Freeride

Freeride is a mix of mountain biking and BMX. It’s all about getting creative on your bike and performing tricks and stunts that blend the two disciplines together. It could be anything from a big jump over a tree, to a flip off a tiny ledge.

Street Riding

Street riding involves riding your bike on streets, sidewalks, and other urban environments. It’s all about performing tricks and stunts such as flips, grinds, and wall rides. It’s also a great way to explore the city and take in the sights.

Slopestyle

Slopestyle is a discipline of mountain biking that combines tricks from BMX and downhill racing. It typically involves riding on a course made up of jumps, rails, and other obstacles. It’s also one of the most popular events at extreme biking competitions.

Dirt Jumping

Dirt jumping involves riding over a series of jumps made up of dirt, grass, and other natural features. It’s an adrenaline-fueled activity that requires great balance and control.

Enduro Racing

Enduro racing combines downhill and cross-country racing, and it’s all about finishing a course as quickly as possible. It’s a great way to test your endurance and skills, and it’s one of the most competitive forms of extreme biking.

Cyclocross

Cyclocross is a form of bike racing that involves riding on a short course that includes obstacles such as jumps, stairs, and sandpits. It’s a great way to test your skills and get a good workout.

MTB Four-Cross

MTB Four-Cross is a popular form of extreme biking that involves four riders racing against each other on a downhill course. It’s a fast-paced event that’s full of thrills and spills.
